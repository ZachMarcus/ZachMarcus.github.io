PROJECTS

NUMakes

Along with a friend of mine, I am starting an organization on campus called NUMakes, whose purpose is to:

  • Prepare students for hackathons and partici­pation in the startup community.
  • Develop students' techincal skills and assist in the organi­zation and partici­pation of hackathon teams.
  • Provide a framework for hackathon discovery and dissemi­nation of related information.
  • Engage students in project development outside of their regular coursework.

If you are at all interested in joining the club (Northeastern undergraduates only, I'm afraid), partnering with us, being a guest speaker at one of our skill workshops, or in any way would like to get involved, don't hesitate to contact me.

Side Channel Analysis

Mobile devices are still incredibly vulnerable to hardware-side attacks. I am working on an Android application and several executables to test this vulnerability on an Android tablet, allowing us to perform timing attacks on the popular RSA encryption algorithm being performed on the device's Snapdragon GPU.

Droidinia

I am working on an Android application to perform benchmark testing on mobile device GPUs.

HP Hack Day

HP posed an unusual challenge: develop a web application using their PaaS (Platform as a Service) to handle everything. We developed a web application to perform k-means clustering on publicly available data, then to display a heatmap using Google's API. The idea here was to make it as easy as possible to add in more data, allowing for the overlay of multiple datasets to observe the relationship between different factors, such as property values and crime rates.

Pavlok Shockathon

The Pavlok wristband was an interesting piece of hardware that was a lot of fun to work with. We ended up creating an application for Android that shocks the user whenever they opened certain applications, such as reddit.
See Pavlok's blog post about it: http://pavlok.com/blog/pavlok-shock-a-thon-leads-to-8-new-api-integrations/

Cleanweb Hackathon

We developed a platform for maintaining a business that would work with recycled wasted plastic into 3D printing filament, as well as exposing its data via a structured API for collection of statistics.

MakeBU Hackathon

This hackathon was my first, my introduction to hackathons and applying any of the knowledge I've gained so far. And after winning, I was determined to not make it my last. For our project, we wanted to reduce the intimidation factor of learning how to play the piano. Our target user was anyone who has ever wished they could play, both young and old. This device was a fraction of the cost of competing devices that can't even provide the same functionality.
